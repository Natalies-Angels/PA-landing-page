Love and Rage

Philia vs Phobia

Philia love is affectionate love, the type that involves friendship. It is the kind of love that strong friends feel towards each other. The rage version is a phobia.

In the context of a remorseful perpetrator of IPV there is a simultaneous journey towards philia â€” a sincere effort to cultivate healthy, respectful relationships marked by deep friendship and affectionate love. This shift is accompanied by an acknowledgment of relationship phobia, an intense fear or aversion to intimate connections arising from past harmful behaviors.

Case study :

Ken, a 27-year-old cis heterosexual male, found himself in the fifth position among six siblings, often feeling overlooked and unloved by his family. Seeking connection elsewhere, he forged a deep friendship with Kendie, a 28-year-old transgender male, who had his own set of challenges. Kendie, the second born in a family of three, witnessed his parents' tumultuous and abusive marriage, leading to his mother's eventual separation from his father when he was 14. The scars of their troubled relationship left Kendie with trust issues and anxiety about forming connections.

The turning point in Ken and Kendie's friendship occurred when Ken, fueled by a sense of betrayal, confronted Kendie over perceived disloyalty. The confrontation escalated into a physical altercation, reflecting the unresolved childhood traumas that both had experienced. In the aftermath, Ken, grappling with remorse, sought support from a group that helped him trace the roots of his anger to childhood wounds. Realizing the depth of his pain and acknowledging the loss of a dear friend, Ken now aims to reconcile with Kendie. He plans to reach out, apologize, and express his sincere desire to rebuild trust in their friendship.
