Love and Rage

Storge VS Astorgos

Storge is familial love. It is the natural love that family members have for one another. It is the type of love that parents feel toward their children and vice versa. The rage version is Astorgos.
Storge in the context of IPV refers to familial bonds, often strained by violence. Astorgos, within IPV, indicates a deficient or absent natural affection, contributing to challenging relationships.

Case study

Born into a family of five with a three-year age difference, Stephen and Stanley navigated a childhood marked by constant competition, especially as the sole cis males. Their father fueled this rivalry, making them vie for everything, even chores. As adults, both became engineers, following their father's advice—Stephen in electrical engineering and Stanley in software.

Stephen, now married and expecting a child, seems to be conforming to societal expectations, but Stanley identifies as aromantic asexual, with no interest in romantic or sexual relationships. His father, oblivious to Stanley's orientation, isolates him during family gatherings, emphasizing his perceived lag in contributing to the family's reputation.

The situation escalates when Stanley's parents arrange a marriage without considering his feelings. Despite his objections, they insist, leaving Stanley contemplating severing ties. Stephen, aware of the arrangement, supports it, along with their three sisters tasked with finding the 'perfect lady.'

Stanley, weary of explaining himself, seeks psychiatric help and discovers his place on the asexual spectrum. Armed with this understanding, he invites his resistant parents to a therapy session, aiming to elucidate the emotional and psychological toll of forced relationships and dates. In this session, Stanley hopes to bridge the gap, fostering understanding and acceptance within his family.
