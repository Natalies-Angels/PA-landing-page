Love and Rage

Agape VS Stoic

Agape is often defined as unconditional, sacrificial love. Agape is the kind of love that is felt by a person willing to do anything for another, including sacrificing themselves, without expecting anything in return. In this case, we focus on love for self. The rage version of it is stoic.
Agape signifies a selfless, compassionate love that emphasizes empathy and prioritizes the partner's well-being. For a remorseful perpetrator seeking change, adopting this approach means prioritizing their partner's safety and emotional health over personal desires. The Stoic approach embodies emotional restraint and suppression, often neglecting the partner's emotional needs. Transforming from stoicism to embracing agape becomes crucial for a remorseful perpetrator, promoting healthier relationships through genuine care and empathy.

Case study :

Sebo, a transgender female, faced adversity in her journey of transition, particularly from her unsupportive family. Born into a family of 5, her father's frequent absence and her mother's traditional views strained their relationship.
Despite their close bond in the kitchen, where they explored recipes and shared gossip, everything changed when Sebo revealed her true self. Her mother's prayers and hurtful remarks pushed Sebo into a self-destructive pattern of repeating her mother's words in the mirror.
Encountering a supportive friend, Sebo began to realize the harm this inflicted and sought solace in a communal home with fellow queers. Attending a coping support group, she learned to affirm herself positively. While Sebo hopes for her mother's understanding, the distance remains, with her mother sending Bible verses, emphasizing the ongoing struggle for acceptance and self-love.
