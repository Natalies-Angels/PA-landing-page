Love and Rage

Eros VS Anteros

Eros is physical love or sexual desire. It is the type of love that involves passion, lust and/or romance. The rage version is anteros.
In the context of IPV from the viewpoint of a remorseful perpetrator, Eros encapsulates the distorted expressions of romantic and passionate love that led to past abusive behaviors. As its opposite, Anteros symbolizes the transformative journey, where the remorseful perpetrator actively strives to replace destructive patterns with reciprocal love. This entails recognizing and rectifying the harm caused, taking responsibility for their actions, and dedicating themselves to rebuilding trust and fostering a healthier, non-abusive dynamic within the relationship.

Case Study :

Ennie, a 35-year-old cis female lesbian, grew up in a challenging environment raised by a single parent who often channeled her struggles into physical beatings and hurtful insults, leaving Ennie feeling inadequate. Despite overcoming these obstacles to become the head of marketing in a top company, her relationship with her mother remains strained, filled with criticism for her life choices. Recently, Ennie met Elle, a non-binary pansexual with a hyper-independent nature, born into a large family. Their relationship, marked by heated arguments and differing conflict resolution styles – Ennie as the pursuer and Elle as the distancer – reached a breaking point when a disagreement escalated into violence. Seeking therapy, Ennie recognizes that her anger stemmed from unresolved issues with her mother. Despite feeling guilty for the violent outburst, she hopes for healing with Elle, who has distanced themselves after the incident. Reflecting on the dynamics, individuals may identify with either the pursuer or distancer role in their own relationships.
